About Me

Background

I grew up in Accra, attending St. Sylvanus R/C School in my early years. The school helped me to realize that the world of technology was advancing.

Alongside my studies, I begun learning C++ and python to build my programming skills. But my time with programming came to a halt when I gained admission into St. Augustine's College. At the college, I was highly engaged in NSMQ (National Science and Math Quiz) activities so I did not have time for programming.
My high school life ended with me leading the college to place second in the NSMQ 2024. With this, I quickly went back to my programming life.

What I Do Now

Currently, I am a first year computer engineering student at Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology-KNUST.
Outside of school, I develop the front-end for mobile applications using flutter. Although, I am still in training, I have the appropriate skills to build a functional mobile app. I am also working on databases using SQL to gain more insight into back-end.

Why I Chose Computer Engineering

I chose computer engineering because it allows me to combine creativity with problem-solving. Engineering is not just about building things; it's about creating solutions that improve lives and push the boundaries of what's possible. The ability to turn ideas into reality is what drives me every day.

Engineering also offers endless opportunities for learning and growth. Whether it's mastering a new programming language, exploring cutting-edge technologies, or tackling complex challenges, there's always something new to discover. This dynamic nature of engineering keeps me motivated and excited about the future.
